CHAPTER I
Biography

Tim Seymour is an award-winning composer and producer based in the New Forest, England. His compositions symphonise rich orchestral heritage with contemporary cinematic production, creating unique and immersive sonic landscapes rooted in emotive, modern British Pastoral melodies.

A graduate of the European Academy of Fine Arts, Tim’s craft has been shaped by elite mentorship from Andy Hill (ex-Disney VP of Music) and orchestration expert Dr. Norman Ludwin. This foundation of high-end musical architecture is bolstered by his professional background in project management (ACMA), ensuring the delivery of world-class cinematic experiences with both artistic depth and technical precision.

Following his 2024 debut classical album, Saviour—a 17-track cycle for string orchestra and piano—Tim’s breakout score for the short film Homologies (dir. by Bryerly Long) earned critical acclaim at the Sherman Oaks Film Festival. This was followed by the score for Lawyered Up (dir. by Tatiana Blackington James), which was showcased at prestigious film festivals worldwide.

A former LSO SoundHub Associate Composer with previous broadcast features on BBC Radio 3, Tim is currently architecting his most ambitious work to date: Tales for All Time. Classically reimagined for modern times, this 15-movement cycle is scheduled for recording with the London Symphony Orchestra at St Luke’s in 2026.
